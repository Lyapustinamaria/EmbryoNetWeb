Drug screening

High-content image-based drug screens can be used to identify novel compounds affecting cellular phenotypes. However, larger drug screens assessing whole phenomes with rich biological information are currently hampered by the large number of images that have to be visually assessed by a limited number of experts combined with the ambiguity of defects and variability between assessors. To validate the general applicability of EmbryoNet in linking chemical manipulations to signaling-based phenotypic defects, we performed a large-scale zebrafish screen using FDA-approved and bioactive compunds.

The screen with 1500 compounds was performed in 96-well plates with four to five zebrafish embryos per well, starting at approximately 2 hpf and imaging every 5 min until 26 hpf. EmbryoNet reliably classified embryos as Dead that were treated with well-known viability modulators with characterized mechanisms of action – such as Aphidicolin, Bafilomycin A1, Blebbistatin, Brefeldin A, Cycloheximide, Cytochalasin, Latrunculin B, Staurosporin, Trichostatin A, Tunicamycin and Vinblastin – whereas mock-treated embryos were classified as Normal Known modulators of signaling pathways – such as the RA agonists All-trans-retinoic acid and TTNPB – were also detected.

Importantly, we identified novel small molecules that had not previously been associated with the regulation of signaling pathways. This includes several hydroxymethylglutaryl-coenzyme A reductase (HMG-CoA-R) inhibitors, a class of compounds also known as statins. Interestingly, EmbryoNet classified embryos treated with several statins including Simvastatin, Atorvastatin, Lovastatin as -FGF. Consistent with the –FGF classification, embryos treated with theses statins consistently displayed defects in dorsal-ventral patterning and broader body axes. Strikingly, the FGF signal transducer pErk was also reduced in Atorvastatin-treated compared to untreated embryos. According to information of package inserts and databases on potentially embryotoxic, teratogenic drugs, it is recommended to avoid the intake of selected HMG-CoA-R inhibitors during pregnancy and lactation period, e.g. Lovastatin. Notably, as substitute substances the intake of Simvastatin is recommended, for which EmbryoNet detected a similar aberrant phenotype class. Our drug screen shows that EmbryoNet can be used to identify teratogenic compounds and associate them with signaling pathways.
